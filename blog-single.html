Supporting Immunity: By Spreading Love

JUN 03
  • by mHealth

Most of us have never experienced the phase of these lockdowns, COVID 19 lockdown is new for all and definitely comes with a lot of anxiety – the anxiety of uncertainty, the unknown or financial stress, etc.. Every individual on this earth is dealing with a different type of anxiety and it will be very wrong to compare each other’s situation.

Suddenly whatever was so important for us has been taken away from us for a while, it can be your favorite coffee shop, your library, your gym, workplace or even home for some of them. We are in a phase of grief and unfortunately, we don’t allow ourselves to feel the grief. It is a process we need to feel each step of it and it’s ok to feel that way or to want to cry. Please do it, only then you will be able to let it go. No pain is big or small, it is your life and your choices.

We are worried because we are thinking more about the things that we cannot control. Focus all your energy and power on the things that you can control and you will suddenly feel the difference, the shift.

Our home will be a place where we will spend most of the time, that will be our environment, try to keep it positive and healthy.

10 tips from my personal experience to stay sane during this phase!
1.Set a routine – plan your day and implement it
2.Learn a new skill – upgrade your knowledge
3.Bring back your creativity – remember you are doing this for yourself, not for anyone else.
4.Try new healthy recipes (If you like cooking)
5.Read a book – if you enjoy it!!
6.Keep your house clean – most important which we may take lightly
7.Create positive surrounding – by using incense sticks, oils or my personal favorite, camphor
8.Play positive music – keep playing any religious chants, nature music or any music that you like in the background.
9.Meditate – even 10 min of self-reflection, journaling or even leaving a voice note to your self will make a difference.
10.Serve others – If you can and get a chance please serve someone in need or your community. That’s one thing we can do to actually live a purposeful life.
