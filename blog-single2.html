Hydrate with foods!

JUN 03
  • by mHealth

Nature has its own way of taking care of us. It has provided us heaps of fruits and vegetables with a high content of water to help us fulfill our fluid intake needs.

According to the Institute of Medicine, 20 percent of your water intake comes from food sources. Many fruits and vegetables, such as spinach, celery, and watermelon, contain 90 percent or higher water content by weight.

Along with drinking water and other fluids, it is advised to add the following fruits and veg to your diet to help you stay hydrated.

Nutrient + Fluid: Dual benefit

One of the most essential minerals is magnesium as it required for performing more than 300 biochemical reactions in the body. Magnesium is important for many processes in the body, including regulating muscle and nerve function, blood sugar levels, and blood pressure and making protein, bone, and DNA. Some foods such as spinach and raspberries gives you both magnesium and water.

Foods such as cantaloupe provide nearly 90% of water along with another important mineral potassium, which is needed for normal cell function because of its role in maintaining intracellular fluid volume and transmembrane electrochemical gradients.

So here we have some of the foods with their water content. Another benefit of choosing foods with high water content is that they will be low in calories. So you can enjoy them without any stress.
