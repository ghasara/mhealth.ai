Exercise for Mental Health

JUN 03
  • by mHealth

We are always told that exercise is good for our health and this is very true for preventing illnesses like cardiovascular disease, retinal disease, diabetes, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease just to name a few! Another thing exercise is good for is mood. This can include aerobic exercise like walking, running, or cycling or slower movements like yoga.

How does it help?


* Yoga increases GABA production and lowers cortisol. GABA is a neurotransmitter that slows your nervous system which can lower anxiety. Cortisol is a stress hormone which is produced when you are in a ‘fight or flight’ response, so lowering this is beneficial for long-term health.
* Yoga also increases brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) which has seen to be decreased in people with depression, so increasing BDNF is thought to lower incidents of depressive symptoms.
* Aerobic exercise like walking, running or cycling has also been shown to increase BDNF. BDNF increases your concentration and memory ability, helping to prevent diseases like Alzheimers.
* After completing a session of aerobic exercise, it shown to be able to lower the blood plasma levels of serotonin, this means it may work via the same mechanisms as SSRI’s (a type of anti-depressant medication) by allowing a higher uptake of serotonin into the brain therefore helping to prevent depressive symptoms. Along with the increased uptake of serotonin, exercise decreases a certain serotonin metabolite (kynurenine) that causes depressive symptoms.

With the COVID-19 situation, it’s an especially good time to try and get out to exercise as it can be a very stressful time and exercise can help alleviate some of that stress.

New to exercise? Don’t know what sort of exercise you’d enjoy? I’ve found some wonderful resources to get you going that include at home yoga, 0-5km running training, walking meditation and a whole bunch of at-home youtube work-out videos.

Recommended amount of exercise per week is 150 minutes (2 ½ hours) of moderate-intensity physical activity, or 75 minutes (1 ¼ hour) of vigorous-intensity physical activity as a minimum to reap the wonderful benefits of exercise.

So, what are you waiting for? Move yourself into a better mood!
